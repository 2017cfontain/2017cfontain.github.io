Capturing the EEG Signals

Two surface-skin EEG electrodes were used to obtain the analog EEG signal. The first was attached to the user's ear, and acted as our reference ground. The second signal electrode was positioned at the back of the head. These two electrodes gave us our input signal that we amplified, filtered, characterized and displayed.

Analog Signal Amplification

In this stage, the signal was amplified nearly 5,000,000x to prepare for filtering and converting to digital.

Analog Signal Processing

Analog filters were used to isolate the signal down to a broad range of interest: 0 Hz to 50 Hz, effectively eliminating power line interference in the process.

Analog to Digital Conversion

While analog signal processing is faster, digital filtersare more precise. In this stage, the processed analogsignalwas converted into an 8-bit digital signal.

  • Read More

Digital Signal Processing

Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) were used to create precise digital bandpass filters, isolating two frequency bands: 9-14Hz for alpha waves and 15-30Hz for beta waves.
